# Product

## Register

product

## Users

**End users (primary — mobile app):** People who want to intentionally disconnect from their devices in a shared physical space (café, library, study hall, co-working space). They open the app, find a nearby quiet zone, commit to a silence period, lock their phone, and earn points. Context: looking for environmental support for a behavior they already value but struggle with alone. Goal: minimal friction to check in, then a beautiful enough last screen they're happy to set the phone down.

**Operators (secondary — web dashboard):** Café owners, librarians, and venue managers who create and manage quiet zones, monitor a live Quiet Index, and offer rewards to participating users. Context: laptop or desktop in a back office or at a counter; not mobile-first. Goal: easy zone setup, at-a-glance live stats, an AI-written weekly digest they can act on without interpretation.

## Product Purpose

Hush turns physical places into measurable, rewarded zones of intentional digital silence. The core metric — the **Quiet Index** (0–100) — is a live aggregate score of how digitally disconnected a zone is right now. Only an anonymized 0–100 silence score ever leaves the device; nothing else. Success: users put their phones down, stay in quiet zones longer, return to the same venues, earn rewards; operators see measurable quiet culture grow in their spaces.

## Brand Personality

Still, spacious, trustworthy. The anti–social-media app. Voice is calm and unhurried — like a good librarian, never a notification. Emotional goal: users should feel the app is genuinely on their side, not harvesting their attention.

## Anti-references

- Any social-media-derived pattern: red badges, count dots, infinite feeds, pull-to-refresh spinners, confetti, streaks as competitive leaderboards.
- Neon cyberpunk, harsh gradients, "war room" dashboards.
- Clinical white-and-blue SaaS — cold, impersonal, corporate.
- Gamified anxiety: anything that makes the user feel they're failing or behind.
- Generic meditation-app look (neutral cream/beige body, thin-weight sans, illustration blob accents, kicker eyebrows on every section).

## Design Principles

1. **Calm over engagement.** One focal point per screen. No stimulation mechanics. The UI should feel like a deep breath, not an obligation.
2. **Negative space is the brand.** *Ma* (間) and *yohaku* (余白) — meaningful emptiness. Margins that breathe. Few elements per screen. Let things settle.
3. **Quiet motion.** Animations are slow, soft, and opt-out. The Quiet Index glow pulses like breathing (~4s ease-in-out). Reduced-motion is a first-class state, not an afterthought — every animation needs a static fallback.
4. **Honest & private.** Visual cues reinforce "we don't watch you." Show the single 0–100 score leaving the device, never raw signals. Trust-building through restraint.
5. **Warm, human, analog-tinged.** Think "a quiet library at golden hour" — soft, slightly tactile, not cold sci-fi. Color carries meaning in exactly one place: the Quiet Index glow.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA (≥4.5:1 body text, ≥3:1 large text). Reduced motion is a first-class state for every looping animation — static or crossfade alternative required; never hide content behind a class-triggered transition. Color is never the sole signal for meaning (the glow is always accompanied by a numeric value). Minimum 44px touch targets throughout the mobile app.
